 >"The death penalty is a means of eliminating pollution... The violent expulsion, the expulsion unto death of the unworthy or accursed individual, has an ancillary sense: that of _devotio._ Indeed, the killing seems in one sense to be an act of piety... The religious function can be seen in the Latin term for these victims: _homo sacer_; as well as in the Greek term _pharmakos_."

Girard, _Violence and the Sacred_, Chapter 11, citing Gernet.
