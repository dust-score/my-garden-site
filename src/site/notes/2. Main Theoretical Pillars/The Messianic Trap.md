When a sovereign claims the authority to decide that the conditions under which the Law was binding have been superseded, they inhabit the role of the [[3. Concepts/Messianism\|messianic figure]].
{ #messiah}


This _[[2. Main Theoretical Pillars/Aufhebung\|Aufhebung]]_ (sublation)—a simultaneous negation, preservation, and lifting-above—that occurs within the conditions of [[3. Concepts/On Finitude\|finitude]] operates as a hermeneutical act: it clarifies the Law's jurisdiction, rendering it inoperative for specific contexts while preserving its structure for future re-engagement.¹ This declaration by a sovereign power of [[2. Main Theoretical Pillars/wolfson-retroactive-not-yet\|messianic time]], consequently a state of exception, is structurally antinomian. It is what Carl Schmitt terms the sovereign exception: a decision that suspends the normative order in its entirety while claiming to stand both inside and outside that order as its ultimate guarantor.² The declaration of "messianic time" is thus a performative speech act that retroactively justifies the suspension of the very Law whose observance was, within the normative model, meant to bring about just that messianic time - the condition of infinitude.
{ #heresy}


The essential flaw in this logic is that messianic time cannot, by definition, be declared from within the conditions of finitude. The claim that it has arrived does not, in fact, transform the condition of finitude within [[3. Concepts/Olam\|reality]]; it merely demands faith in the redeemer's sovereign authority. The believer is required to hand over their moral and interpretive agency to the judgment of one who claims to stand beyond the Law. This produces the condition Agamben terms [[2. Main Theoretical Pillars/bare life\|bare life]]: existence exposed to [[1. Surface Texts/Jobcenter Texts/Power\|power]] without the protection of a normative order.³ 

This is what I understand as exteriorised messianism: the abdication of the messianic function to a redeemer figure, arbiter of truth, and ultimate authority. What does it mean, by contrast, for the subject, the comparatively powerless individual, to internalise the messianic? Agamben's _via passiva_ is one answer: the rendering inoperative within the domicile of the self of the forces which would act upon it, and by extension refusing the reproduction of the machinery of their production. This is, however, a mode of agency representative of that last [[3. Concepts/reshimu\|trace]] of autonomy available to the subject of _bare life_. My personal position is not that limited.

To profane, in Agamben's sense, is to return what has been relegated to the sphere of the sacred back to common use.⁶ This is the interiorised messianic act of [[3. Concepts/tikkun-as-inhabiting-retroactive-not-yet\|Tikkun]] that the agentic subject performs: the abolition of the Law within the conditions of the self which performs an _Aufhebung_ of the purview of sovereign power. This renders the machine of the reproduction of the conditions of separation between human and sacred inoperative: an internal state of exception.
{ #profanation}


In the Midrash of Moses ascending to receive the Torah, the angels object to a creature born of woman receiving the Law. Moses does not claim sovereign authority over the angels. He demonstrates by an act of interpretation that a Law addressing murder, theft, and adultery has no purchase on beings for whom death, property, and desire are irrelevant categories.⁷ This is an internal _Aufhebung_: Moses both understands the necessity of the Law for human life and establishes his non-beholdenness to the angels' claim of jurisdiction over it. He renders the angels' objection inoperative not by decree, but by exposing its categorical inapplicability. The act of _Tikkun_ here is precisely this: the [[3. Concepts/implication\|implication]] of the sacred into its proper context of use, the discernment of its true shape and function within the constraints of finitude. The interiorised messianic subject perfects the world not by rushing towards conditions of collapse, but by refusing the shortcut of sovereign decree. This requires an ongoing engagement in the labour of distinguishing the Law's life-giving order from the structures that falsely claim its authority.
{ #moses}
