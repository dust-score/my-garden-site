Agata Bielik-Robson, in Tarrying with the Apocalypse, describes the apocalyptic ideation endemic to Western thought. It is a cultural framework for finitude which all too often falls prey either to optimism and pessimism in the face of the assumed “end of history”. I am interested in that which exists above the two, perhaps best conceptualised as Nahman’s scream or Agamben’s _messianicity_.
