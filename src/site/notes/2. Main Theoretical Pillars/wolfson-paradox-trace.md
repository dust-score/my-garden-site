>Insofar as the trace is the capacity for boundary within the boundless, a capacity that the boundless must contain as a facet of its perfection, it follows that we are led logically  to an infinite regress, the paradox of the point of the trace, which cannot be  disentangled from the trace of the point, that is, the trace of infinity in which  there can be no trace that is not itself the trace of a trace, a nonphenomenal  trace of what cannot be incorporated within the either/or economy of absence or presence, the erasure that is the inception of writing, the concealment of  the concealment that is prior to there being anything to conceal, the timeless  point that propagates the encircling line that is time.
