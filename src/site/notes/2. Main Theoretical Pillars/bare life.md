> "The protagonist of this book is bare life, that is, the life of _homo sacer_ (sacred man), who may be [[2. Main Theoretical Pillars/Gernet\|killed]] and yet not _[[5. Citations/Violence and the Sacred\|sacrificed]]_."

— Giorgio Agamben, _Homo Sacer: Sovereign Power and Bare Life_, trans. Daniel Heller-Roazen (Stanford University Press, 1998), p. 8.

> "Bare life remains included in politics in the form of the [[2. Main Theoretical Pillars/The Messianic Trap\|exception]], that is, as something that is included solely through an exclusion."

— Ibid., p. 11.
