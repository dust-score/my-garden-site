As opposed to Luzzato, who adopted what Magid[^1] calls a more acosmic stance - viewing the experience of God’s absence as an illusion to be overcome by means of devotion, which could culminate in an experience of communion with God - Nahman approached the paradox of faith despite the experience of its contradiction ==phenomenologically / dialectically==.

[^1]:  Shaul Magid, Through the Void: The Absence of God in R. Nahman of Bratzlav's Likkutei MoHaRan
