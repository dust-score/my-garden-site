[[2. Main Theoretical Pillars/Theory Notes/nahman-intro\|Nahman]] and Luzzato present us with divergent possibilities for relation to the experience of finitude (i.e. the experience of the absence of God). Luzzato’s approach represents the strategy of reconciliation with [[3. Concepts/Nahman On Paradox\|paradox]]: we are perspectivally incapable of knowing [[3. Concepts/Olam\|truth]], therefore it behooves us to dedicate ourselves to action as aligns with our desired reality. 

In the face of the unanswerable question, heretical thought is a dangerous potential consequence of the engagement with the paradox of the experience of God’s absence. Heresy, understood more broadly as the wilful abnegation of moral codes, may be understood as the inverse of _simple faith_: as we are perspectivally incapable of knowing truth we may as well spend our lives in the furtherance of our own egoic interests.

WRONG: This is what Nahman, without condescension, called _[[2. Main Theoretical Pillars/Theory Notes/simple faith vs. dialectical faith\|simple faith vs. dialectical faith]]_. 

Nahman’s scream, conversely, represents what I will call [[3. Concepts/implication\|implication]]. If the experience of the absence of God[^2] tempts heresy, and the maintenance of the belief in the immanence of God is mindless faith, Nahman's proposition is that of a non-resolution, a kind of sublation. If it is accepted that paradox is an aspect of existence and will never be resolved, the paradox can itself become an aspect of faith, thereby reconstituting existence within finitude. 

In this sense, the experience of God’s absence may be folded into the self through the techne of 1) the enactment of [[3. Concepts/tsimtsum-nahman\|tsimtsum-nahman]] within the self and 2) the embodiment of paradox, and its [[3. Concepts/implication\|implication]] through the scream. In this way the [[2. Main Theoretical Pillars/Theory Notes/deleuze on the grey dot leaping over itself\|"leaping over"]][^1] of the paradox becomes as much a part of reality as is the experience of the paradox, i.e. of finitude.

[^1]: See Deleuze, On Painting, p. 20

[^2]: or other factors contraindicative of that which had been construed to provide life with meaning

[[3. Concepts/tsimtsum-nahman\|tsimtsum-nahman]]

1. **Yearning**: An existential response to the experience of God's absence.
    
2. **Zimzum of the heart**: The contraction of unbridled desire, creating an empty void within the heart, just as God contracted to create the cosmic void.
    
3. **The scream**: A wordless, primal cry that breaks through the silence of the void, acting as a "midwife" for a new consciousness.
    
4. **Prayer (words)**: Articulate speech now emerges from the contracted, emptied heart, corresponding to the divine creative utterances that fill the void.
    

> "The scream brings one past the darkness, through the void to the presence of God as creator by giving birth to a new state of consciousness, moving from the wordless to the word." (Magid, 507)

**B. The Danger of Incomplete Process**

Rectification fails if the process is not completed. There are two dangers, corresponding to the two opposite poles:

> "First, one must not allow the emotions embedded in one's heart to go unattended, thus avoiding confrontations of divine absence. This is the realm of eyn sof where creation, prayer and divine service (avodah) are impossible. Second, one must avoid only confronting the emptiness and not completing the creative process by actually elevating the yearning for God in prayer. The first danger will lead to antinomian heresy... The second danger will yield to fatalism: the individual will become overcome by the absence, seeing no possibility of redemption, and thus will be trapped in the snares of the second heretic." (Magid, 510-511)

**C. The Dialectical Role of Reason**

Reason cannot be abandoned. The _zaddik_ must pass _through_ the heretical questions, not avoid them:

> "In R. Nahman's dialectical faith, reason—depicted as the experience of the absence of God—is represented by the second heretic... In this case, reason cannot be avoided but must be confronted and only then overcome." (Magid, 517)

### Thesis 6: The Scream as the Infolding of Infinitude into Finitude

The scream, in R. Nahman's spiritual physiology as interpreted by Magid, is the wordless, pre-articulate cry that breaks through the silence of the void and enables the birth of prayer. It is the point where the _zimzum_ of the heart—the contraction of unbridled desire—crosses into articulated speech.

> "The scream serves as a midwife, as it were, which, reflecting the exilic character of the shekhina, yields a new level of consciousness... The scream brings one past the darkness, through the void to the presence of God as creator by giving birth to a new state of consciousness, moving from the wordless to the word." —Magid, 505-507

Conceptualised through Wolfson's analysis of the trace, the scream is the **inscription of the _reshimu_**. It is both retention (what remains after the contraction of unbridled desire) and presage (the portent of the prayer that will follow). It is the "nonphenomenal trace" that gives what it cannot give—the givenness of the nongiven. The infinite is not represented in the scream; it is _implicated_—folded in—as the constitutive absence that the scream makes present precisely as absence.

> "The intentionality of the imagination is to be distinguished from that of perception insofar as the givenness of the perceived object has the character of actuality, whereas the reproductive givenness of the imagined object is characterized as fictive, and in this sense, it can only be given as nongiven." —Wolfson, 13

Conceptualised through Deleuze's reading of Klee's grey point, the scream is the act that seizes the nondimensional chaos-point and affixes it as centre, causing it to "leap over itself" into the matrix of dimensions. The scream is the cosmogenetic act: one hand in the pre-pictorial (pre-verbal, pre-articulate) condition, the other hand within the order of articulation. It is the grey point becoming the egg. **Tikkun as infolding** is precisely this: the nondimensional paradox of the void, affixed and centred by the act of the scream, leaping over itself into the order of creation—not resolved, but transposed, implicated into a new register.

> "The first grey point is nondimensional... The second one is the same, but the same in another form, at an entirely different level, at another moment. This time, it's the grey point that's become center, henceforth the matrix of dimensions, to the extent that it is established, that is: between the two, it has leaped over itself." —Deleuze, Lecture on Klee

The scream is thus a mode of _tikkun_ as **infolding**: the implicating of the non-articulable within an articulation, the folding of infinitude into the finite act.
