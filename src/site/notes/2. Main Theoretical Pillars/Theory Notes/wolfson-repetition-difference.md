Wolfson delineates compossible thought on the matter of repetition in motion through time constituting novelty, in Derrida and [[2. Main Theoretical Pillars/Theory Notes/deleuze-repetition\|Deleuze]]. 

_Here, “the principle of repetition “is no longer that of the Same, but involves the Other—involves difference, from one wave and one gesture to another, and carries that difference through the repetitive space thereby constituted._
