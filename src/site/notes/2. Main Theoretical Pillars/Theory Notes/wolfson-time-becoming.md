>To the extent that becoming marks the being of time, we can conjecture that the facticity of the latter is such that nonbeing and being coalesce, not as the dialectical resolution of antinomies but as the paradoxical juxtaposition  of contraries that belong together in virtue of their intractable disjuncture. As Merleau-Ponty put it, “Past and future exist all too well in the world, they exist in the present, and what being itself lacks in order to be temporal is the nonbeing of the elsewhere, of the bygone, and of tomorrow. Past and future voluntarily withdraw from being and pass over to the side of subjectivity, to seek there not some real support, but rather a possibility of non-being that harmonizes with their nature.

Retroactive Not Yet, Linear Circularity and Kabbalistic Temporality, Elliot R. Wolfson

> "Time is the measure of this incommensurability, the beckoning of the eternal will that materializes in the temporal enfolding of the middle." —Wolfson, 28
