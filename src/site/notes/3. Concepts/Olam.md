the Hebrew root **ע-ל-ם** (*ʿ-l-m*) carries two interconnected meanings:

---

**1. עוֹלָם (_olam_) — "world," "eternity," "cosmos"**

This is the sense you're working with in your thesis: time as circular linearity, the retroactive not yet, the space-time in which creation unfolds and tikkun operates.

**2. עָלַם (_alam_) — "to hide," "to conceal"**

A verb from the same root. In biblical Hebrew, it means to hide something or to be hidden. The connection is semantic as well as morphological: what is eternal or cosmic is also, in some fundamental sense, _hidden_. The world conceals as much as it reveals. Divine presence is obscured by the very structures that sustain creation.

---

## The Kabbalistic Implication

The _olam_ is the realm of concealment. Ein Sof is hidden within the _olam_ — contracted, veiled, accessible only through the sefirot and the vessels. Creation is simultaneously revelation and occlusion: "the nonmanifest cannot be manifest without being occluded," as your Wolfson quote has it. The world (_olam_) is the hiding (_he'lem_) of God.

This is directly relevant to your thesis. The Jobcenter is an _olam_ — a world, a maintenance velocity, a system. And it hides what it maintains. Dusty arises from what's hidden in plain sight. Implication is the practice of unfolding what the _olam_conceals. The fold is where hiddenness and revelation meet.
