Here, then, I find it gainful to employ Agamben’s[^1] secularised [[messianicity\|messianicity]], the mode of agency of the subject of _[[2. Main Theoretical Pillars/bare life\|bare life]]_, the symmetric opposition to _[[2. Main Theoretical Pillars/The Messianic Trap\|sovereign power]]_. If sovereign power is that which decides on the establishment of the state of exception,  messianicity is the implementation within the self or oppositional community of an interiorised _aufhebung_: the moment in which something does not become, but is, possible.


[^1]: Derrida
