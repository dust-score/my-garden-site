Paradox can here be read as related to finitude in that it is an experience of the latter. 

In [[2. Main Theoretical Pillars/Theory Notes/nahman-intro\|R. Nahman of Bratzlav]]'s thought, the experience of paradox derived from two separate, albeit related, sources. The first is the [[3. Concepts/The Realm of non-absolute Paradox\|The Realm of non-absolute Paradox]] that is of the aspect of the failure within finitude, which is of the [[1. Surface Texts/On Creation\|breaking of the vessels]]. This is the experience of ineptitude, of the chasm between ideal and real and the limitations in bridging the complexity of reality through limited subjectivity, whether in scientific, moral, philosophical, or other thought. 

> "The first challenge confronting the one who seeks divine wisdom is the heresy rooted in the [[3. Concepts/On Finitude\|finite creation]], the product of the breaking of the cosmic vessels in [[1. Surface Texts/On Creation\|Lurianic Kabbala]] (_shvirat ha-kelim_). In this heresy, as in the finite world, God exists, and by finding the divine element in the philosophical question one can resolve the crisis of faith."

Magid, [[5. Citations/6. Papers/Magid - 1995 - Through the Void The Absence of God in R. Naḥman of Bratzlav's Likkutei MoHaRan\|Through the Void]] p. 502

> "In the realm of these difficulties, it is not external science that encounters the demand of religion, but if it is possible to say so, religion itself contradicts itself there. One religious demand pushes aside another religious demand."

Weiss, [[5. Citations/6. Papers/Weiss, The Empty Space; Sabbateanism and its Melodies\|Weiss, The Empty Space; Sabbateanism and its Melodies]]

[[3. Concepts/The Empty Space\|The Empty Space]], which is the consequence of [[3. Concepts/tsimtsum\|tsimtsum]], before the introduction of any bridging between the resultant [[1. Surface Texts/One Story of Finitude\|finitude]] and the retracted infinitude, is a condition of possibility or potential but not manifestation. The second type of paradox arises from this, the question of the _possibility_ of finite existence within infinitude, or of infinitude (divinity) _within_ finitude.

> "The non-existence of God within the world and His existence within it despite everything ... is the desperate contradiction of the world's structure. How is it possible that there is no God within the worlds and yet there is?"

Weiss, [[5. Citations/6. Papers/Weiss, The Empty Space; Sabbateanism and its Melodies\|Weiss, The Empty Space; Sabbateanism and its Melodies]]

> "The question of the second heretic cannot be resolved rationally. ... The void is not a lacuna between the two dimensions of God, but the possibility of the nonexistence of the transcendent God, which makes the immanent God an illusion."

Magid, [[5. Citations/6. Papers/Magid - 1995 - Through the Void The Absence of God in R. Naḥman of Bratzlav's Likkutei MoHaRan\|Through the Void]], p. 503

It is the second type of paradox which Nahman, according to Magid in _Through the Void_ (1995), faces with the [[2. Main Theoretical Pillars/Theory Notes/Implication of the Scream\|scream]]. In [[5. Citations/6. Papers/Weiss, The Empty Space; Sabbateanism and its Melodies\|Weiss, The Empty Space; Sabbateanism and its Melodies]], Weiss does not engage with Nahman's scream. Here, the tzaddik, who is [[2. Main Theoretical Pillars/The Messianic Trap\|of the aspect of Moses]], and therefore of [[2. Main Theoretical Pillars/Theory Notes/Silence, Song and Screaming\|silence]], faces the ultimate "difficulty", which is also of the aspect of silence - the unutterable absolute paradox - with their own silence. In both the silent response and the scream, we see an [[3. Concepts/implication\|implication]] of the nature of the paradox. It is not held at a distance, to be studied or resolved intellectually. This is of course clearly impossible. It is also not put aside. Rather, it is [[3. Concepts/On Folding\|folded]] into the self. It is worth noting that in both cases it is emphasised that the tzaddik is of the aspect of Moses. In Nahman's framework, the tzaddik who is of the aspect of Moses carries [[3. Concepts/Messianism\|messianic]] implications. This is because he performs an action isomorphic to Moses' ascent on Sinai, bridging the unbridgeable, bringing articulation back from the inarticulable.
