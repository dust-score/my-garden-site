Jacques Derrida, "Shibboleth: For Paul Celan," in _Sovereignties in Question: The Poetics of Paul Celan_, edited by Thomas Dutoit and Outi Pasanen (New York: Fordham University Press, 2005), p. 50: "[T]he incommunicable secret of the Judaic idiom, the singularity of 'his name, his unpronounceable name' that is 'his truth' and that 'can only be foreign' and 'outside'." Derrida further elaborates, ibid., pp. 50–51: "The date is a shibboleth only if it is circumcised. Circumcision is a shibboleth, it is the shibboleth. The date is circumcised or it is not a date. The poem is circumcised or it is not a poem. The poet is circumcised or he is not a poet. The Jew is circumcised or he is not a Jew." On the motif of circumcision in Derrida's reading of Celan, see the discussion in Wolfson, _Giving Beyond the Gift_, pp. 279–280.
