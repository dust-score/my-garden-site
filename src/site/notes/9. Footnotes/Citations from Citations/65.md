Celan, _Gesammelte Werke, Dritter Band_, p. 193. The English translation is from Paul Celan, _The Meridian: Final Version—Drafts—Materials_, edited by Bernhard Böschenstein and Heino Schmull, translated by Pierre Joris (Stanford: Stanford University Press, 2011), p. 10. For discussion of this passage in the context of the Meridian speech, see Derrida, "Shibboleth," pp. 9–10; Christopher Fynsk, "The Realities at Stake in a Poem: Celan's Bremen and Darmstadt Addresses," in _Word Traces: Readings of Paul Celan_, edited by Aris Fioretos (Baltimore: Johns Hopkins University Press, 1994), pp. 159–184; and the detailed analysis in Pierre Joris, "Celan's Counter-Psalm: Translation, Circumcision, and the Poetics of the Meridian," in _Paul Celan: Studies in His Early Poetry_, edited by Christine Ivanović (Stuttgart: Klett-Cotta, 2008), pp. 39–56.
