On the trope of the Jew as wanderer, see the sources cited in Elliot R. Wolfson, _The Duplicity of Philosophy's Shadow: Heidegger, Nazism, and the Jewish Other_ (New York: Columbia University Press, 2018), p. 221 n. 85. The linkage of the wandering Jew and the poet is a theme that runs deeply through Celan's corpus. For a representative sampling of scholarly discussions, see the references in Wolfson, _Giving Beyond the Gift_, p. 420 n. 207.
