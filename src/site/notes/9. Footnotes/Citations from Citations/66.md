The connection between Tsvetaeva's dictum and Celan's Meridian is discussed extensively by Derrida, "Shibboleth," pp. 47–55. On the broader implications of the shibboleth as a cipher of linguistic and ethnic identity, see the analysis in Marc Redfield, _Shibboleth: Judges, Derrida, Celan_ (New York: Fordham University Press, 2021), particularly pp. 1–23 and 107–141. See also the discussion of the shibboleth motif in the context of Jewish identity and poetic language in Shira Wolosky, "The Ethics of Mourning: The Role of Materiality and the Poetic in the Work of Paul Celan," _Germanic Review_ 74 (1999): 269–289.
