Let there be no heaven and no earth,  
peace down to the last sparrow’s cry,  
the dearly beloved white horses are also  
on speaking terms with eternity,  
  

and all because you would not pay  
me as your prophet my just deserts  
and all because not a penny  
of boiling money comes from, goes to, me;  
  

Yes, all stands in the autism of ages  
when every greeting is a coin high in the sky  
shimmering over the madness of your tides  
when the lost white horse surges from the breaker,  
and all stands as before, my prophetic calling  
scuds from the molten letter Gimmel.

  
In the scuds of the molten letter Gimmel  
I sought well-being, and found it not:  
the Succot branches under an open sky  
the language that no hot iron breaks  
  

the foaming on the tongue, transfigured  
like the burden of memory into prayer  
the bitter herbs in the palace  
as in the hut of authority  
  

the bitter peace on the kerbstone  
torn from side to side by the host people  
the nebbish in pyjamas in the madhouse  
roared at by the conscience of clouds  
and ringed by fire in the eyes, otherness  
of the faithful, death in the rafters.


(Amos Weisz)
