_[[3. Concepts/Eyn Sof\|Eyn Sof]]_ intended to experience Godself. To do so, it had to become less and also more than Godself: allow for something that was not God, that would nevertheless become _through_ God, as God was all that was. If God was infinity, how could anything be that was not God? God contracted, and this was _[[3. Concepts/tsimtsum\|tsimtsum]]_. Through tsimtsum, God made space for creation. This did not alter the infinite nature of G-D. Within the vacated space (_[[3. Concepts/The Empty Space\|ḥalal panui]]_) was left the _[[3. Concepts/reshimu\|reshimu]]_, the impression or trace, the subtle remnant of divine light, which holds the potential for creation, like the trace of ink on a paper after the letters have been erased. _Reshimu_ becomes the substance of the vessels, _[[3. Concepts/shevirat ha-kelim\|shevirat ha-kelim]],_ which are formed of the trace and are to hold Ein Sof’s light. Therefore, the _kelim_ are formed from the _reshimu_ (trace) of divinity within the _The Empty Space_ of its absence, and from it the vessels were made. Afterward the _qaw_ (line - the controlled reintroduction of divinity) came into it and from it was made the _aẓmut_ (essence). This is the difference between the essence and the vessels, for the vessels are from the aspect of the contraction (mi-beḥinat ha-ṣimṣum), and the essence is the secret of Ein Sof, which enters into the vacated space (ḥalal ha-meṣumṣam)_._ This is the bridging of in/finitude; as Wolfson states in The Retroactive Not Yet, “the main goal of the withdrawal is to produce the vessels that will reveal the light by concealing it, since the nonmanifest cannot be manifest without being occluded.” This is a notation of the necessity of limitation, ergo finitude, in the creation of the world.

_“The potential for finitude is thus coiled within the folds of infinity – it could not be otherwise because the inclusivity of the infinite is such that it must possess even the capacity to be exclusive, the capacity to be less than infinite.” _

The vessels, which were created of the aspect of divinity’s capacity for finitude, were structurally weak in the initial phase of _[[1. Surface Texts/Tohu_ (chaos)\|Tohu_ (chaos)]] because each existed as an isolated point without interconnection. When the divine light, which was of the aspect of infinitude, entered them, they could not bear its intensity and shattered. This is _shevirat ha-kelim_ — the breaking of the vessels. The shards of finitude (_kelipot_), which now held sparks of divine infinitude (_nitzotzot_), fell through the void. The fragments of infinitude trapped and obstructed within finitude cannot return to the divine unity without the work of Tikkun.

After the breaking of the vessels (_shevirat ha-kelim_), Eyn Sof repeats the process of emanation. In the second, rectified creation (_[[3. Concepts/Olam\|Olam]] ha-Tikkun_), the vessels are reconstituted as interlocking structures (_partzufim_), each containing the others and thus able to share the burden of divine light without shattering.

These partzufim are not mere attributes but dynamic, interacting personalities, capable of “[[3. Concepts/Olam\|enclothing]]” themselves within one another, as a soul becoming enclothed in a body. It is through their interactions that the work of [[1. Surface Texts/TIKKUN\|Tikkun]] proceeds: the scattered divine sparks from the shattered vessels of Tohu, now trapped within the lower worlds of _Beri’ah_ (Creation), _Yetzirah_ (Formation), and _Asiyah_ (Action), are gradually elevated and restored to their source. This elevation is not accomplished by divine fiat but through [[1. Surface Texts/Tikkun Olam\|human]] agency: the performance of _mitzvot_ (commandments), mystical _[[1. Surface Texts/kavanot\|kavanot]]_ (intentions), and [[1. Surface Texts/the rectification of one’s own soul\|the rectification of one’s own soul]], which is mirrored in the divine realms above.
