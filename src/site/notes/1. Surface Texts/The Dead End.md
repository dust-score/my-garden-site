*If you want to step into infinitude, just go in all directions into the finite.*

_J.W. Goethe_

Whether the dead end can be satisfactorily conceptualised as the impossibility of the  reconciliation of opposing narratives or of bearing the unbearable, there is a dead end. It is not singular in its presentation: though they may grow from a distant and singular body, all I see are the many heads of the Hydra. This body then is the unutterable and inexplicable, as each head tells me a different tale. It requires an engagement with the unanswerable, that which causes untenable tension when engaged. This is a tension that can immobilise movement, suspend between interpretations, overwhelm with complexity, and confound narrative.
